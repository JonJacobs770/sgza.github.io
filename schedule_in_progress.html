Opening Keynote: Nurturing Diversity in Technology Through Lean Startup & Product Kata

Natalie Warnert

Nurturing Diversity in Technology Through Lean Startup & Product Kata (case study: Women in Agile Org)

The Lean Startup and Product Kata mindset of small, targeted experiments is a great way for products to determine how to expand meaningfully and sustainably while examining data and experience collected along the way. These tools can also be expanded to organizations that provide non traditional products such as services, information, and other types of messaging to target their audiences' needs in the right ways. One example of this is the Women in Agile movement, which started like most products do, with curiosity about a problem. Now it has grown to a non-profit organization with local groups across the world and many conference pairings under its belt all in a timeframe of a few years. Natalie will discuss the Lean Startup and Product Kata themes using Women in Agile as a case study. Learn how these product frameworks were used to expand a movement, that does not provide a physical "product", to get to where it is today, a global non profit with over 4000 members, and how you can take our lessons learned to nurture your curiosity, organizations, and non traditional "product" ideas in a successful and sustainable way.

Learning Outcomes
  • Understand how to implement the Product Kata to design experiments and analyze data/metric changes in your target problems to solve
  • Learn to apply the Lean Startup and Product Kata concepts to services and organizations/movements (versus traditional physical or software products)
  • Articulate the Women in Agile story as a non traditional movement built and cultivated through curiosity, experimentation, and decentralization

The Life Changing Magic of Holding Space: A Stance for Decluttering Your Organisational Space

What is Quality and How Does it Emerge Within an Agile Organisation?

- Louise Perold

What is Quality and How Does it Emerge Within an Agile Organisation?

Are your customers happy with your product? Do you have a large on-call team to support production? Do you have a long lead time for any change due to the massive testing effort required? Does your team panic about making changes? Do you have a war room? How can quality emerge in your context?

As I have experienced different contexts and been responsible for testing within them, I have grown more and more aware of factors that influence our ability to embrace quality as part of the team culture. In this workshop, I will introduce and use task analysis to:

  • understand what might be getting in the way of a quality culture
  • uncover potential levers within our teams that could be shifted / changed
  • identify experiments to run and what we should observe to help us optimise for quality
Learning Outcomes
  • What quality is and what is important for quality to emerge;
  • What factors (context drivers) might be influencing quality in your context;
  • Feedback loops that can be included;
  • Frames or factors that can be adjusted to allow quality to emerge through:
    • Observations
    • Reflections
    • Diagrams
    • Discussions

Facilitating High Stakes Retrospectives

- Kirsten Clacey
- Bee Sharwood

Facilitating High Stakes Retrospectives

What does a retrospective of only C-suite members look like? How does running a retrospective at the end of a 1 year project add value to the organisation? Most Facilitators/Scrum Masters feel comfortable in the team retrospective space. We know our humans, we know our context and this familiarity lends itself to feeling relatively comfortable and safe (most of the time). However, some retrospectives feel quite different. We want to zoom in on what we call High Stakes retrospectives, looking at what makes a retrospective feel “high stakes” and suggesting a model for facilitators to navigate these spaces.

Learning Outcomes
  • Knowledge of a basic framework that will increase the impact of retrospectives outside of Scrum teams
  • Comprehension of some of the factors which complicate organisational level retrospectives

Beyond Tech: Confusion, Conflict, and Lost Productivity

- Riaan Nel

Beyond Tech: Confusion, Conflict, and Lost Productivity

We find ourselves in a complex world where technology and business collide. Between business and IT, we don’t speak the same language, we don’t always see things in the same way, and we have different needs – but ultimately, we work towards the same goals.

This talk is about effectively navigating your way through our complex world and bridging the gaps that lead to confusion, conflict, and lost productivity. I’ll draw on my experience working at a number of different companies of varying sizes, and talk about some of the common issues that I’ve seen when business and IT teams work together – and how we can solve those issues.

Spoiler Alert: Users are not the enemy, even when they give you grief.

Learning Outcomes
  • Understand the importance of a common understanding across business and technical teams.
  • Understand what motivates other people that you work with.
  • Understand how to communicate effectively.
  • Understand the value of close collaboration.
